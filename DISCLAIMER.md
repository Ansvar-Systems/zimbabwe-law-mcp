## Data Authority and Limitations

### Source Authority

This Tool aggregates data from official Zimbabwean legal databases. However:

| Source Type | Authority Level | Use Case |
|------------|----------------|----------|
| **Official government databases (Government Gazette)** | Authoritative | Statute text (primary reference) |
| **ZimLII (Zimbabwe Legal Information Institute)** | Supplementary | Legislation, case law, cross-references (verify independently) |
| **International treaty databases** | Authoritative for international law | International obligations metadata |

**CRITICAL**: ZimLII and other open-access resources, while generally reliable, are **NOT** official government publications. They may contain transcription errors, outdated information, or incomplete coverage.

### Commercial Legal Databases

For professional legal work, **always cross-check with authoritative legal resources** such as:

- Veritas (veritas.co.zw), LawHub Zimbabwe, Zimbabwe Law Reports (ZLR)

These services provide editorial oversight, comprehensive annotations, and professional-grade currency guarantees that this Tool **cannot** provide.

## Currency and Staleness

### No Real-Time Updates

- Database updates are **manual** and may lag official publications by **weeks or months**
- Zimbabwean law changes continuously through new Acts of Parliament, Statutory Instruments (SIs), constitutional amendments, and court decisions
- **Last-updated timestamps** in tool responses indicate data age, but should be treated as unreliable

### Staleness Warnings

The Tool includes staleness warnings when data is >30 days old. **These warnings are not exhaustive**:

- Even "fresh" data may be outdated if recent amendments were published in the Government Gazette
- Staleness warnings do not guarantee completeness or accuracy
- Always check official sources for the current legal position

### Amendments and Repeals

- A provision marked "in force" may have been:
  - Amended after the last database update
  - Repealed but not yet removed from the database
  - Subject to transitional provisions or savings clauses affecting its applicability
  - Affected by constitutional amendments (Zimbabwe has had multiple constitutional amendments)
- The Tool does **NOT** automatically track amendments in real-time

## Coverage Gaps

### What's Missing

This Tool does **NOT** include:

1. **Complete case law** -- Coverage is limited; do not rely solely on this for case law research from the Constitutional Court, Supreme Court, High Court, or lower courts
2. **Historical statute versions** -- Limited availability of historical provision wording
3. **Legal commentary** -- No editorial annotations, academic commentary, or practice guides
4. **Unpublished decisions** -- Lower court decisions and administrative rulings largely absent
5. **Full legislative history** -- Limited coverage of Hansard (parliamentary debates) and explanatory memoranda
6. **Statutory Instruments** -- Coverage of delegated/subsidiary legislation (SIs) may be incomplete
7. **Customary law** -- Customary law applicable in Zimbabwe is not covered

### Impact on Professional Use

These gaps mean:

- **Incomplete Legal Research**: Tool results are inherently incomplete and may miss critical authorities
- **Context Missing**: Without commentary and legislative history, interpretation may be incorrect
- **Customary Law Blind Spots**: Zimbabwe's legal system incorporates customary law, which this Tool does not cover
- **Constitutional Interpretation**: Rapid constitutional development means Tool data may not reflect current constitutional jurisprudence
- **International Law Blind Spots**: Cannot fully assess international treaty obligations or compliance
